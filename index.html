Safe Drone Racing With an Unknown Payload

Brandon McDonald · Kaleb Ben Naveed · Dimitra Panagou

A delivery drone has to fly fast while staying inside a safe zone it can prove it will not leave, even when carrying something it has never felt before. Every run below is recorded from the simulator.

Now turn the gatekeeper off

The safety check (the gatekeeper) is a system that ensures the drone stays within a safe zone. Here the empty drone is told to lap the chicane faster than its bends really allow, and the run is recorded twice: once with the gatekeeper, once without it. That switch is the only difference between the two runs.

How the gatekeeper decides it is safe to go fast

The drone never outruns its escape plan. Before each stretch of racing, the gatekeeper rehearses the whole way out: brake, drift back to the middle, crawl. If the rehearsal leaves the safe zone, that stretch is too long and gets trimmed. The faint red crosses mark where the unchecked drone flew out.

Every run here is recorded from the simulator: a bare drone that holds its safe zone, the same drone dragged out by an unknown swinging load its safety check cannot feel, and one too-fast lap flown twice, with the gatekeeper on and off. The loads are deliberately heavy. Teaching the check to feel the load, so it stays both safe and fast, is the next step.
