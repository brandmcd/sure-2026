These are real runs from the gatekeeper simulator, not illustrations. Open one and press play: the quad banks and tilts the way the real attitude does, the strip along the bottom turns green where the safety check commits and red where it refuses, and the charts track how far off the line an unknown payload drags it. Reading a run: the bottom strip is the safety check over time, one block per tick, green where it commits the racing plan and red where it falls back to the safe stopping maneuver. The charts show the disturbance the model has to learn and the distance off the line against the corridor wall. Everything is in simulation on the full nonlinear drone.

All results are in simulation on the full nonlinear drone. The safety guarantee is conditional on the sets we check, not absolute. Payload presets are deliberately heavy and provisional. The task-by-task build is mine; the broader active-learning vision is the lab's direction.
